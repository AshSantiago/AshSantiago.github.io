# About Me

I am a researcher studying **Golden Eagle ecology, survival, and population dynamics**.

My work focuses on uncovering potential drivers of population decline using a combination of telemetry, statistical modeling, genomics, and bioinformatics. My goal is to bridge research and management to provide actionable conservation strategies to our partners that will have the greatest impact on population health. 

## Research Interests

- Raptor ecology
- Survival modeling
- Population dynamics
- Conservation Genomics
- Bioinformatics
- Bayesian statistics in ecology

## Background

I received my B.S. degree in Zoology from University of Wisconsin - Madison before spending five years working education and conservation technician positions focused on long-term monitoring. I became invested in using long-term monitoring data to help develop conservation strategies for declining populations. This led me to pursue a position in the Heath Lab at Boise State University where I have been researching the Golden Eagle population in southwestern Idaho since 2021. This project is the perfect combination of a fascinating study system, beautiful landscapes, incredible commmunity of people, and opportunities to make a real difference.
